Analisa Bauhaus: Resumap

Welcome to my resumap! In addition to my standard resume, I created this resume-map to give you a better idea of who I am and where I come from.

Scroll through to see how I got to where I am today.

The Beginning - San Carlos, CA

I was born and raised in San Carlos, California by my mother, an educator and my father, a tech company executive. They instilled me with values of inclusion, academic excellence and doing good for people and the planet.

High School: 2010 to 2014 - Redwood City, CA

From 2010 to 2014 I attended Sequoia High School in Redwood City, California. I was a club swimmer, and also swam and played water polo for my high school. I was a member of the California Scholarship Federation and an All-American Athlete for both swimming and water polo.

College: 2014 to 2018 - Carlisle, PA

In August of 2014 I moved thousands of miles away from home to Carlisle, Pennsylvania to continue my education at Dickinson College. In my second semester at Dickinson I was invited to join and initiated into Kappa Alpha Theta, a Women’s Fraternity with values of intellectual curiosity, leadership potential, commitment to service, and personal excellence. During my sophomore year, I joined the Red Devil Swim Club, Dickinson’s club swim team and was made co-president. I was also the club’s representative on the Sports Club Council.

Summer Abroad: 2016 - Málaga, Spain

I spent the first part of the summer before junior year in Málaga, Spain as part of Dickinson’s Summer Study Abroad in Spain program where I practiced Spanish daily and developed a deep love for Málaga and Spanish culture.

Summer: 2016 - San Francisco, CA

After returning home to California from Málaga, I interned for Environmental Entrepreneurs (E2) in San Francisco. At E2 I helped in advocating for California environmental policy through data input, policy briefs and meeting with California state senators in support of CA Senate Bill 32, which expands on the 2006 Assembly Bill 32 to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. In September 2016 the bill passed!

Semester Abroad: 2017 - Málaga, Spain

In Spring 2017, I was able to return to Málaga for a semester abroad. I spent 5 months living with a Spanish host family and attending classes at the Universidad de Málaga. By the end of my stay in Spain, my Spanish fluency had improved immensely along with my understanding of Spanish culture.

Summer: 2017 - Harrisburg, PA

In the summer before my senior year at Dickinson, I was able to have two different internships. The first was with the Pennsylvania Behavioral Health and Aging Coalition (PBHAC) where I was a Communications Intern. At PBHAC I managed their social media presence, event marketing and coordination and attended trainings to develop better healthcare for older Pennsylvanians.

Summer: 2017 - Carlisle, PA

The second internship I had in the summer of 2017 was with Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) located in the courthouse in Carlisle. There, I managed various case files and attended court hearings where CASA and Children and Youth Services were involved while also learning first-hand the challenges children in our communities face every day.

Graduation: 2018 - Carlisle, PA

In May 2018, I graduated from Dickinson College in Carlisle, PA with a Bachelor of the Arts degree in Sociology and Spanish Language and Literature!

Today - Harrisburg, PA

I am currently doing a year of national service through the AmeriCorps VISTA program. AmeriCorps VISTA is a national poverty alleviation program that sends volunteers into professional settings to work on projects targeting issues of poverty. I am serving as a Marketing and Communications Associate at the Community Action Association of Pennsylvania working on a set of marketing resources designed to help the public and Pennsylvania's Community Action Agencies talk about issues of poverty in a concise, compelling way.
